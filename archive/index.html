The sun has not moved since you arrived. No one else on the road seems to find this strange — though you have gathered that night, when it finally comes, will last four months.

Ahead, in the shade of an old tree, sits a broad-shouldered figure — grey-whiskered, ape-like, robed, a lap full of scrolls and a kettle warming beside him.

He watches you approach with the patience of someone who was expecting no one, and is pleased anyway. He gestures to the stone beside him.
